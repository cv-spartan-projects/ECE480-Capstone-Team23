Development

From concept to system

The project evolved through simulation, breadboard validation, PCB design, and full integration, sponsored by Texas Instruments to demonstrate the INA351A in a complete, portable application.

Phase 1
The Goal
Design a compact ECG signal acquisition and display system sponsored by Texas Instruments. The system must capture a real ECG signal, process it through an analog front end, and display the waveform in real time on an LCD, operating as a standalone, portable demo platform built around the INA351A instrumentation amplifier.
Phase 2
Component Selection
A weighted decision matrix evaluated key subsystems. The TI MSPM0G3507 was chosen for its TI ecosystem alignment, ultra-low power, and sufficient ADC resolution. An LCD module was selected over OLED/TFT for clarity, simple interfacing, and low cost. The INA351A was a fixed TI requirement as the primary device under demonstration.
Phase 3
Signal Chain Design
Full ECG signal chain designed: INA351A for instrumentation amplification (gain 20), followed by an OPA333 gain stage reaching ~1000 V/V total. A passive RLD connection stabilizes body potential. The system runs on a single 3.3 V supply with a 1.65 V virtual reference (VREF) buffered by an OPA333, allowing the bipolar ECG signal to swing correctly without a negative supply rail.
Phase 4
0.6–33 Hz Passband
Sallen-Key HPF and LPF stages were tuned experimentally using swappable through-hole components on the breadboard, enabling rapid real-time frequency testing. Final values establish a passband that removes baseline drift and high-frequency noise while preserving the PQRST complex.
Phase 5
MCU + LCD Integration
Firmware developed for ADC sampling at ~250 Hz, real-time waveform rendering, and touchscreen control. A state machine enables navigation between the main menu, live mode, and simulated mode. ADC polling ensures each conversion completes before the sample is processed. A pause feature relieved SPI bandwidth pressure when switching screens.
Phase 6
KiCad Custom PCB
Two PCB variants designed in KiCad: one with a 3.5 mm AUX jack for gel electrodes, one with exposed copper fingertip pads. Both use the same signal chain. Component placement follows signal flow with analog/power separation, close decoupling caps, and a full ground plane. Multiple iterations incorporated sponsor feedback. Due to fabrication timing, system validation was completed on breadboard/perfboard.
Phase 7
Enclosure & Final Build
Two enclosures developed: an acrylic open-display platform for transparent demos, and a 3D-printed enclosure with hexagonal ventilation for a product-like form factor. A branded NANUK 910 hard case provides rugged transport with MSU and TI logos. The final system captures and displays live ECG waveforms running fully from a USB power bank.
Challenges

Problems we solved

Six significant engineering challenges encountered and resolved over the course of development.

Hardware
Single-Supply Bipolar Signal
ECG signals are bipolar but the system runs on a single 3.3 V supply. Fixed by creating a 1.65 V virtual reference (VREF) and biasing the entire signal chain around it, allowing the ECG to swing above and below VREF without clipping.
Hardware
Filter Cutoff Tuning
The waveform would slowly taper flat, indicating an incorrect HPF cutoff. Swappable through-hole components on the breadboard allowed rapid experimental tuning to find the correct 0.6 Hz high-pass cutoff.
Hardware
VREF Stability
Multiple stages drawing current from the resistor-divider VREF caused voltage drift and signal instability. Adding an OPA333 buffer to the VREF node created a stable, low-impedance reference for the full signal chain.
Hardware
Electrode Interface
The circuit worked with a function generator but produced unstable real-human signals. Gel electrode pads were adopted for validation; copper fingertip pads were used in the final design for portability.
Firmware
ADC Conversion Failures
No ADC values were initially displayed on the LCD. Implementing ADC polling (having the CPU wait for each conversion to complete before processing) resolved the issue and ensured reliable sample capture.
Firmware
Touchscreen + Waveform Conflict
SPI bandwidth was fully consumed by waveform rendering, blocking touchscreen inputs. A waveform pause feature freed the SPI bus to process touch events and enable proper screen navigation.

How to Use This System

You scanned the QR code and landed here. Follow these steps to set up and operate the ECG Signal Acquisition & Display System from scratch. No prior experience needed.

⚠️
Important: This is a demonstration and educational device only. It is not a medical device and is not intended for clinical diagnosis, treatment, or medical use of any kind.
01
Open the carry case
Locate the hard shell carry case. Lay it flat on a stable surface and open the lid. Everything you need for a full demonstration is stored inside.
02
Identify what's inside
When opened, you will see all components neatly stored. Confirm you can locate: the acrylic platform system, 3 ECG electrode gel pads, 2 power banks, 2 USB-A to Micro USB cables, and the electrode cable with colored connectors.
03
Remove the necessary components
Take out the following from the case: the acrylic platform system, 3 ECG electrode gel pads, 1 power bank, 2 USB-A to Micro USB cables, and the electrode connector cable. Set them out on a flat surface.
04
Apply the electrode gel pads
Peel and apply the 3 ECG electrode gel pads to the correct positions on your body as shown in the photo. Place one on your right arm, one on your left arm, and one on your right leg. Press firmly to ensure good skin contact.
05
Power on the system
Insert the power bank into its 3D-printed slot on the acrylic platform. Plug in both USB-A to Micro USB cables, both USB-A ends go into the power bank: one Micro USB into the AFE board, and one Micro USB into the MCU LaunchPad. Press the button on the power bank to turn it on. The LCD will display a welcome screen confirming the system is powered up.
06
Connect the electrode cable to the AFE board
Take the electrode connector cable and attach each colored clip to the corresponding electrode pad on your body using the color coding below. Then insert the male AUX connector from the cable into the female AUX input on the AFE board.
Yellow, Left Arm
Red, Right Arm
Green, Right Leg (Right Leg Drive)
07
Navigate to Live ECG mode
From the welcome screen, tap the LCD screen once to enter Live mode. The screen will turn blue and display the word "LIVE" confirming you are in live mode. Use your fingernail when tapping the touchscreen for best responsiveness.
08
View your live ECG signal
Tap once more to begin displaying your live ECG data in real time. Your heart's electrical signal will appear as a scrolling waveform on the LCD screen. Hold still for the best signal quality. Motion creates noise artifacts.
Screen Navigation Reference

Complete tap guide

🔴 Live Mode

  1. From the welcome screen, tap once → blue "LIVE" screen appears
  2. Tap once more → live ECG waveform begins displaying
  3. Press and hold at any time → returns to welcome screen

⚫ Simulation Mode

  1. From the welcome screen, double tap → red "SIMULATION" screen appears
  2. Tap once → raw simulated signal (stored on MCU)
  3. Tap once more → clean simulated signal (stored on MCU)
  4. Tap once more → loops back to "SIMULATION" screen
  5. Press and hold at any time → returns to welcome screen
📍
Signal quality tip: Ensure the gel electrode pads are firmly adhered to clean, dry skin. Minimize movement while viewing live data. If the signal looks noisy, check that all three electrode connections are secure at both the pad and the cable connector ends.
